No Pre-Funding? How Global Payments Without Pre-Funding Means Less Risk and Better Cash Flow

Discover how global payments without pre-funding reduce risk and improve cash flow by keeping your working capital accessible until the moment you need it.

When businesses send money abroad, there is often one phrase that causes hesitation: pre-funding foreign accounts. On the surface, it sounds like a normal part of global payments. But in reality, it is one of the main reasons many businesses face frozen cash flow and unexpected risk.

Let's break it down. Pre-funding means you deposit your own money into an account before sending it to your overseas partner or vendor. In other words, your funds sit idle, sometimes for days, while banks or middlemen process the transfer. That is your working capital, locked away, not earning, not helping your business grow.

Now imagine a better way: no pre-funding required. That is exactly what ZilRemit.com offers.

What Does "No Pre-Funding" Really Mean?

Traditionally, businesses that send international payments must deposit funds into a foreign account before making a transaction. This is called "pre-funding." It is meant to ensure liquidity on the other end, but it ties up your money, sometimes for days, before it even reaches your supplier or partner.

With Zil Remit's no pre-funding model, you only pay when you actually make the transfer. There is no need to park funds in a separate account overseas. That means your money keeps working for you until the very moment it is needed.
